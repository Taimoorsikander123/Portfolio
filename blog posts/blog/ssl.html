SSl

SSL stands for secure socket layer. SSL is a technology used to secure data between two connections (Two connections can be a webpage and it’s user). SSL makes it impossible to see the data transferred between two connections. SSL secures sensitive information by using encryption algorithms. For example, during online shopping, all your financial and personal information is protected by SSL. TSL (Transport Layer Security ) is a modified form of SSL. But still, SSL is a commonly used term. In TLS, encryption is used to secure important data, the same as in SSL. During online searches, some websites are shown by URL http:// while others with https:// . The URL starting with https:// shows that the website is protected by an SSL layer.

Encryption

Encryption is encoding of sensitive information to protect your privacy from unauthorized users. A Cipher is generated using letters, numbers and symbols. Characters in Cipher are standing in for original data. No one will be able to see your data without a decryption key. Encryption has two types. Symmetric encryption is the first type, and it has the same encryption and decryption key. Asymmetric encryption differentiates itself from symmetric encryption, as encryption and decryption keys are different. Encryption is a very essential part of data privacy. A huge part of private information is shared online including personal and financial. Organizations having private data must use encryption and other methods to protect their users' information. Digital Security is gaining more importance as we are banking and shopping online.

SSL Certificate Authority

Prefixes HTTP or HTTPS are a matter of great concern when you are talking about secure websites. Online shopping businesses and other governmental organizations always use SSL secured websites. Before using your information online, it’s advisable to check (that website is secured by SSL. SSL Certificate Authority (CA) are organizations that provide SSL certificates. CAs are trustable organizations that help to protect websites and its users' privacy. Main duty of CAs is to issue digital certificates and set up trust between the website and its users. CAs issue private keys and public keys. Private keys are confidential (even not shared to CAs). CAs take a fee to complete the process. Digital certificates issued contain information about the organization like name, contact information, public, expiry data and more. Digital signatures are signed by CAs on Digital certificates.
